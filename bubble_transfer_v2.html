The risk never disappears — it transfers. Each Fed rescue pushes cheap money into a new sector. That sector inflates. When it pops, the Fed cuts rates again. Each cycle, the bubble is larger and more dangerous than the last. The underlying structural problem is never fixed — only deferred. This is the mandate's fatal flaw.
